Santanu Soe

I am a Prime Minister's Research Fellow (PMRF) and Joint PhD scholar in the Department of Mathematics at IIT Madras and the School of Mathematics and Statistics at the University of Melbourne, working under the supervision of V. Vetrivel and Matthew K. Tam. I have also received the Melbourne Research Scholarship for my doctoral studies at Unimelb.

My research focuses on designing and analysing algorithms that are both theoretically sound and computationally efficient. I am particularly interested in operator splitting methods, primal-dual algorithms, and monotone inclusion problems.
